Donald Trump

Political Orientation

Donald Trump is affiliated with the Republican Party, which is a right-leaning party. His key policy proposals include bringing back the economy, protecting US manufacturing, implementing an 'America First' foreign policy, building a border wall and curbing immigration, lowering drug costs and healthcare reform, promoting US energy, and defending the Second Amendment. He has also proposed tax cuts, stricter immigration policies, increased military spending, and does not believe in climate change. He does not support cutting Social Security or Medicare benefits and supports limiting restrictions on guns. He has an extensive immigration plan that includes building a wall at the Mexican border, deporting undocumented individuals with criminal records, and temporarily banning some migrants from entering the United States. He wants to repeal and replace Obamacare, limit and ban abortion at some point in pregnancy, and significantly reduce income taxes for most Americans. He also aims to lower individual and corporate taxes, cut regulations, end trade deficits, undo Obamacare, cut drug prices, reform Medicaid, and get and keep the economy reopened. He has challenged international alliances, embraced adversaries, limited access to abortion, imposed a ban on transgender troops in the military, protected Second Amendment rights, withdrawn from the Paris climate accord, and boosted fossil fuel development.

Economic Policy

Donald Trump's proposed economic policies included individual and corporate tax cuts, attempts to repeal the Affordable Care Act, trade protectionism, immigration restriction, and deregulation. He also responded to the COVID-19 pandemic. His policies resulted in increased federal budget deficits and the national debt. The number of uninsured Americans increased, and his tax cuts were projected to worsen income inequality. The impact of his policies on healthcare costs and coverage was mixed. Repealing the ACA would have adverse consequences. The 2017 Tax Cuts and Jobs Act primarily benefited higher-income taxpayers and increased the budget deficit source 1.

In terms of infrastructure, Trump sought an infrastructure bill worth $2 trillion and mentioned winning the 5G race and establishing a national high-speed wireless internet network. On taxes, Trump wanted to extend the individual income tax provisions included in the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act and proposed tax credits for companies that bring jobs back to the U.S. On healthcare, Trump proposed spending cuts to Medicare and Medicaid and aimed to lower healthcare insurance premiums and end surprise billing. In terms of trade, Trump pursued a trade war with China and aimed to put America first in all dealings with countries. On jobs and wages, Trump aimed to create jobs through infrastructure investments and wanted to continue the "Buy American, Hire American" immigration agenda. On climate change, Trump did not believe in climate change and rolled back environmental rules. Finally, on student debt, Trump proposed eliminating the Public Service Loan Forgiveness program and replacing the four income-driven repayment programs with a single one source 3.

However, Trump's handling of fiscal policy has been criticized for increased spending and a growing deficit. His trade policies, particularly his use of tariffs, have been viewed as detrimental, resulting in higher prices for imported goods, reduced U.S. exports, and increased economic uncertainty.
